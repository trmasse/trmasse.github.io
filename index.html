Trevor Masse's Yuval Noah Harari Book Course Creation Project Overview

Assignment Specification

Design, build and evaluate one week of an online course, training, or experience. It should be fully functional for a hypothetical student or participant, with the exception of content requiring extensive media production.

Personal Goals

As an Online Learning Manager for HBS Online, I work at the intersection of education, technology, and business. Together with my own interests in philosophy and history, Yuval Noah Harari's 2024 book Nexus: A Brief History of Information networks from the Stone Age to AI stood out as a timely, professionally relevant, and fascinating subject matter. The course I created, Understanding Information and AI: Yuval Noah Harari's Nexus provides learners with an introduction to the major arguments and concepts from the book to show how information affects human society, including important changes that are being caused by (generative) Artificial Intelligence. I chose to use the Articulate Rise LMS for this project since I had not used it previously.

Course Format

  • Asynchronous, Self-Paced
  • No Instructor Presence
  • No Pre-requisites, assignments, or grades
  • One module, featuring 7 sections
  • Overall course completion estimate of one hour

Audience

The course is envisioned as a supplement to an undergrad course on AI, anthropology, or philosophy (college or adult learners). It would also be of interested to those who have read Yuval Noah Harari's books - 45 million sales worldwide.

Design and Pedagogical Principles

  • Curation of book content and Narrative writing, with a small number of related concepts presented in each section
  • Each section begins with one or two guiding questions, to introduce themes and draw on learners' prior knowledge
  • Following the same overarching structure as the book, the sections are scaffolded from the more broad and theoretical to the more specific and technologically-focused
  • Brief Check Your Knowledge sections at the end of each lesson helps cement the learner's understanding before progressing
  • Short videos of interviews with Yuval Noah Harari provide entertaining recaps and further develop main ideas from the course
  • Chapters of Nexus are suggested as optional activities for those who are reading the book alongside the course
  • Each lesson uses the same visual aesthetic, with important quotes emphasized in large breakout-style sections
  • Photographs are used throughout to create a stylish and modern LMS course experience
